What is Colic?

Colic is a common condition in infants where they cry for extended periods without an obvious cause. It usually starts a few weeks after birth and often improves by the age of 3-4 months. Though it's not harmful, it can be very distressing for both babies and parents.

Signs and Symptoms

  • Intense crying episodes, often in the evening
  • Clenched fists and arched back while crying
  • Difficulty being consoled
  • Flushed face and tight abdomen

Tips for Parents

  • Hold and soothe the baby gently
  • Use white noise or soft music
  • Try tummy time and gentle rocking
  • Check for feeding issues or allergies
  • Don't hesitate to seek support from healthcare professionals

Our Mission

As nursing students from 2BSN-D, Group 5 – Colic, our goal is to raise awareness about colic and support families by providing accurate, compassionate, and helpful information.
